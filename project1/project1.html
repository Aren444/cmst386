SEO

How Search Engine Optimization Works

To boost visibility online, designers have to make sites their best so they appear in relevant keyword searches. Properly naming page directories and structuring content helps search engines organize it. Google specifically follows three steps: crawling (finding content), indexing (saving it), and serving (showing results to users). Duplicate content can hurt rankings, so originality is important.

Keywords and Meta Tag Descriptions

Using keywords effectively is essential for a website's success but can be harmful if misused. Quality, relevance, and a customer-first mindset are key. Keywords should appear in the title and meta description, but never be overused. Descriptive URLs and alt text support both accessibility and SEO. Most importantly, ensure users are satisfied with what they find. It was especially helpful to learn that Google no longer uses meta tag keywords for ranking, so focus on meaningful content over stuffing.

4 Elements of Relevant Content Links

Four key elements of writing relevant content links include: (1) creating original, helpful, and well-organized content; (2) ensuring links lead to reliable, updated sources; (3) making your site worth visiting through good design, speed, and mobile-friendliness; and (4) using internal and external links strategically to boost visibility, encourage crawling, and improve search engine indexing.

Good Heading Hierarchy

Good heading hierarchy uses relevant, concise, and informative titles arranged in a clear semantic order to help both users and screen readers. The main topic of a page uses an (h1), followed by subtopics in (h2) to (h3) headings (or more up to h6 as needed). For example, a page titled Healthy Life Habits would have h1 to h3 for heading, topics, and sub-topics:

MY EXAMPLE:


Healthy Life Habits (h1)

Eating Healthy Foods (h2)

Fruits (h3)

  • Watermelon
  • Oranges
  • Blackberries

Nuts (h3)

  • Almonds
  • Macadamias
  • Pistachios

Vegetables (h3)

  • Carrots
  • Avocados
  • Cucumbers

Growing Hearty Kids (h2)

Preschool (Ages 3-5) (h3)

  • Planting easy-to-grow flowers or herbs
  • Simple cooking projects like making fruit kabobs
  • Storytime with character-building books

Elementary (Ages 6-10) (h3)

  • Building a birdhouse together
  • Creating a gratitude journal
  • Volunteering for local community clean-ups

Pre-Teen (Ages 11-13) (h3)

  • Organizing a small fundraiser for charity
  • Learning basic budgeting and saving money
  • Participating in group sports or team projects
